Social Capital Atlas Report

16 September, 2022

Summary

This report derives from the Social Capital Atlas project developed in collaboration with Meta, Opportunity Insights, and researchers from Harvard, New York University, and Stanford 1. These data are nested at the highschool level. Using 21 billion riendships found from publicly available Facebook account information and the National Center for Education Statistics (NCES) data, SES and identify social capital constructs in students were analyzed2. Measured variables include:
  • Baseline measures of SES found by combining proxies for SES (e.g., median incomes in ones residential ZIP code, cell phone model, college attended, etc.) observed on Facebook.
  • The constructs of connectedness, cohesiveness, and civic engagemennt3.

This document is in reference to Lincoln HS in the zip code 68510. There are currently 2184 students enrolled at Lincoln HS. Of these students, 8.3% of students volunteer.

Share of high parental SES is defined as: two times the share of high-parental-SES friends (who attended the same school within three birth cohorts of the individual; high SES is quantified as being above the median income) among high-parental-SES individuals, averaged over all high parental-SES individuals in the school.
